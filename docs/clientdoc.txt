# What I Want: A Support App for Ev Bus

*Hey, thanks for sitting down with me. Let me explain what I'm after — I'll just talk it through the way I think about it in my head, and you tell me what's realistic.*

---

## The short version

I run a customer-facing business in Ghana. Right now my customers message me on WhatsApp, call my personal phone, and email — and it's chaos. I want **my own support app**. Something my customers log into, send me a message, and one of my team replies. Like WhatsApp, but it's *ours*, it's branded "Ev Bus", and it's organized so nothing gets lost.

No tickets. No "your call is important to us". Just a conversation.

---

## Why I'm building this

Let me be honest about what's broken right now:

1. **Customers DM me personally on WhatsApp.** At 2am. On weekends. And if I don't reply in 5 minutes they think I'm ignoring them. I'm sleeping.
2. **My team has 4 support guys.** When a customer messages, none of them know who's supposed to answer. So either everyone answers (chaos) or nobody does (customer gets mad).
3. **When one of my guys is sick or quits**, his customers just stop getting replies. We don't even realize for days.
4. **I can't prove anything.** Customer says "you promised me a refund last Tuesday." Did we? Who said it? No record.
5. **Onboarding new customers is manual.** Someone wants access, they WhatsApp me, I check their info, I say yes or no. No trail, no consistency.
6. **When we do a promotion, hundreds of people message at once** and we drown.

I want all of this to stop being my problem.

---

## How it should work — from the customer's side

A customer goes to our website. They see a clean page that says something like:

> **"Support that actually feels human. Get a direct line to our team — no tickets, no queues. Just a conversation."**

They click **Get Started** and sign up with their **name, email, Ghana phone number, and a password**. Optional: they can attach a short note — "I'm signing up because my last order arrived broken" — with a photo.

Then they wait. Their account is **pending**. They see a message: *"We're reviewing your account, you'll get an email when you're approved."* That email comes from us, looks professional, has our logo.

Once approved, they log in and — boom — there's their **chat thread** with us. One thread. It stays open forever. They type, we reply. They can:

- Send **text, photos, documents** (PDFs, Word files).
- See when we're **typing**.
- See **blue ticks** when we've read their message.
- **React with emoji** to our messages (👍, ❤️, 😂 — you know).
- **Reply** to a specific message (quote it, like WhatsApp).
- **Delete** their own message if they sent it by mistake (within a few minutes).

If one of my agents **closes the conversation** ("Hey, your refund is processed, all sorted 🙏"), the customer sees a banner and a closing note. But they can **reopen it** anytime with one click — "Actually it's not sorted, here's what happened." Full history stays.

When they're not in the app and we reply, they get a **notification on their phone**. And an **email** if they've been away for a bit. But not spammy — if we send 5 messages in a row, they get ONE email saying "you have 5 new messages", not five separate emails.

Also — and this is important — it has to **work on their phone browser**. Most of my customers don't have laptops. They're on Android, on mobile data, sometimes on slow connections. The app has to be fast and light. Ideally they can **"Add to Home Screen"** and it feels like a real app, with push notifications and everything. But no app store — too much friction.

## How it should work — from my team's side

Each of my support guys logs in and sees a **dashboard**. The most important thing on it is the **list of conversations**.

Here's what I want that list to feel like:

- **Whoever's been waiting longest is at the top.** If a customer sent a message 30 minutes ago and nobody's replied, they should be screaming red at the top of the list. I don't want my team "cherry-picking" the easy ones.
- **Unread counts** next to each name.
- **A little status** — is this customer mine? Unassigned? Being handled by Kwesi?
- The **last message preview** so they can see context without opening it.

When they click into a conversation, it should feel exactly like WhatsApp. Real-time. They type, the customer sees them typing. They send, the customer sees it instantly. No refresh button anywhere.

### The part I really care about — **smart assignment**

This is the bit I need you to get right. When a new customer sends their first message, the app should **automatically decide which of my guys handles them**. Not randomly. The logic I want:

1. **Pick someone who's online right now.** No point assigning to a guy who went home.
2. **Pick whoever has the fewest active conversations.** Balance the load. Don't give the new guy to my star agent who's already juggling 20 people.
3. If we have **departments** (say, one guy handles Accra, another handles Kumasi), route based on that.
4. **Don't bother me (the boss) unless everyone is drowning.** Only escalate to a Super Admin — me — when all my regular guys are, say, 80% full.
5. If **nobody's online** at all (it's 3am), queue it and grab someone the moment they log in.

And then there's the worst-case scenario: **what if Kwesi quits tomorrow?** His 15 active customers shouldn't just vanish. The app should **automatically reassign all his conversations** to the other guys the moment I suspend his account. I shouldn't have to do it by hand. That would be a nightmare.

### What my guys need to do

- Reply to customers (obviously).
- **Send photos and documents** back to customers.
- **Close/archive** a conversation when it's resolved, with a short note that gets emailed to the customer.
- **Grant a customer permission** to upload files (by default they can't, to prevent spam).
- **Reset a customer's password** if they forgot it — the app emails them a temporary one.
- **Kick a customer out** if needed (suspend them).
- See when their teammates are **online or away**.
### My guys also need to talk to EACH OTHER
Two ways:
1. A **team group chat** — all the agents together. "Anyone seen this error before?" / "Boss, the payment system is down." Internal, customers never see it.
2. **Private direct messages** between two agents. "Yo Kwesi, can you cover my shift?"
Same WhatsApp feel — typing, read receipts, reactions, the works.

## How it should work — from MY side (the boss)
I'm a **Super Admin**. I can see and do everything my guys can, plus:
- **Approve or reject** new signups. With a reason if I reject. The customer gets an email either way.
- **Suspend** a customer or an agent.
- **Create new agent accounts** for new hires.
- **Reassign** any conversation to any agent — override the auto-assignment.
- **See every conversation in the system**, not just mine.
- **Post announcements** that go out to everyone.
- **See a full log** of who did what, when. If someone says "who suspended this customer last Thursday?", I can look it up. Who approved them, who reset their password, who closed their conversation — all there.
- **Configure everything** without calling you. Message limits, file size limits, how many devices someone can be logged in on, the brand colors, the homepage text, the FAQ — all of it should have a settings page I can edit myself. And the changes should take effect **immediately**, everywhere, no restart.
---
## Announcements
Sometimes I need to talk to all my customers at once. "We're doing maintenance Saturday night." Or "New promotion: 20% off this week!"
I want to write an announcement — title, body, maybe an image — and choose who sees it:
- **All customers**
- **All my agents** (internal announcements)
- **Everyone**
Customers can **upvote or downvote** it (so I know if my promotions land), **react with emoji**, and **comment**. I can set an expiry date so old ones disappear.
Some announcements I want to also show on the **public homepage** (before people log in) — like a banner about a new service. My regular agents should NOT be able to do that — only me. And agents should only be able to message customers, not each other (I don't need them plotting 😄).
Offline customers get a **push notification** when I post.
---
## Reports

Two kinds:

1. **At signup**, a customer can attach a note — "Here's the problem I'm having." That's a **registration report**. When I approve them, it should follow them into their conversation so whoever handles them sees the context.
2. **After they're approved**, customers can file **new reports** anytime — "My order is broken again", with a photo. My team sees it, investigates, and marks it **Resolved**. The customer sees the status update live.

I want a **report queue** my team can work through, sorted by newest, with a badge showing how many are pending.

---

## Files and photos

- Customers can upload **images** (JPEG, PNG, that sort of thing) and **documents** (PDF, Word).
- Limits: let's say **5MB for images, 10MB for documents, max 50 uploads per day per person.** I want to be able to change these myself.
- **Strip the location data out of photos** when they upload. I don't want my customers' GPS coordinates sitting in our files — that's a privacy disaster waiting to happen.
- **Check the actual file**, not just the extension. Don't let someone rename `virus.exe` to `photo.jpg` and upload it.
- **Don't store the same file twice** — if two customers upload the same promo image, store one copy.
- **Documents should be private.** No public links. If someone needs to view a document, give them a temporary link that expires.

---
## Notifications — this is make or break
I cannot overstate this. **If notifications don't work, the whole app is useless.** My customers will not sit refreshing a webpage. Here's what I need:
- When they're **in the app and looking at the chat** — no notification needed, they see it.
- When they're **on another tab or the app is in the background** — **push notification** on their phone/desktop. "New message from Ev Bus: Hi Kofi, your refund is processed..."
- When they're **fully offline** for a while — an **email**. But smart: if 10 messages come in 30 seconds, send ONE email saying "You have 10 new messages", not ten emails. And never delay a notification more than, say, 5 minutes.
- If a customer has **turned off email notifications** in their settings, respect that.
- **Password reset emails and approval confirmations are special** — those send immediately, no debounce. When someone clicks "forgot password", the email should be in their inbox within seconds.
And — here's a big one — **I want a backup email provider.** Right now I'm thinking Brevo, but if Brevo goes down for an hour, I want it to automatically fall back to Gmail. I never want to get a support ticket saying "I never got my password reset email." That's the most embarrassing support ticket a support company can get.

## Security stuff (I don't know the technical words, but...)
- **Strong passwords.** Don't let people use "password123". Minimum 12 characters, mix of letters, numbers, symbols.
- **Don't store passwords in plain text** obviously — hash them or whatever you do.
- **Limit how many devices** someone can be logged in on. Say, 2. If they log in on a third phone, the first one gets kicked out.
- **One click "log me out everywhere"** if they think they're hacked.
- **Rate limit the login** — if someone tries the wrong password 10 times, lock them out for half an hour. Don't let people brute-force.
- **Don't store people's IP addresses forever** — I think there's some GDPR rule about that. Anonymize them or truncate them or whatever.
- **Log everything important.** Who logged in, who approved whom, who deleted what. If something goes wrong, I want to be able to trace it.
---
## Reliability
The app **cannot go down**. I know that's unrealistic, but hear me out:
- If the database has a hiccup, **retry**, don't just show an error.
- If the email provider is down, **queue the emails** and send them when it comes back.
- If the file storage breaks, **stop trying for a bit** (don't melt the server) and **tell me** — show a warning on my dashboard.
- **Graceful shutdown** — if you need to restart the server, finish what's in progress. Don't drop a customer's message mid-send.
And I want a **health dashboard** I can look at: is the database responding fast? Is storage working? How much memory are we using? How many people are connected right now?

## Subsidiaries / departments
We might grow into branches. Accra office. Kumasi office. Maybe a "Billing" department and a "Tech Support" department. I want to be able to:
- Define these departments in settings.
- **Assign each agent to one or more departments** (or leave them as "generalist" who handles anything).
- When a customer comes in, **route them to an agent in the right department.**

## Performance
- It has to feel **instant**. Like, genuinely instant. No spinner when I send a message.
- It has to handle **500 people signing up in an hour** when we run a promotion, without falling over.
- It has to be **cheap to run.** We're a Ghanaian business, we're not made of dollars. I don't want a $500/month AWS bill. Find the cheap option.
- **Mobile data is expensive here.** Don't make the app download 5MB every time someone opens it.
---
## What I DON'T want (so we're clear)
- ❌ **Voice or video calls.** Too much bandwidth, too complex, not what we do.
- ❌ **A public community forum.** This is private 1-on-1 support.
- ❌ **E-commerce or payments.** We handle payments elsewhere.
- ❌ **An AI chatbot.** The whole point is HUMAN support. If I wanted a bot I'd use the form on my website.
- ❌ **A mobile app in the app stores.** Too much friction for customers to install. Browser + "add to home screen" is enough.
- ❌ **Multi-language** for v1. English only. We'll add Twi later if it takes off.
- ❌ **A ticket system.** I hate tickets. That's the whole reason we're building this.

## The dream
Picture this: A customer, Kofi, signs up at 9pm Tuesday. By 9am Wednesday I've approved him — he got a nice email, he's excited. By 9:05am he's chatting with Ama (one of my agents) who picked him up automatically because she was online and had the lightest load. Kofi's problem is solved by 9:20am. He's happy. Ama's happy. I'm happy.
Six months later, Kofi comes back with a new question. He opens the app, and there's his whole history with us — every message, every promise, every photo. Nothing lost.
Meanwhile, I'm looking at my dashboard and I can see Ama's handling 12 conversations today, Kwesi's out sick but his 8 customers got auto-reassigned to the others, nobody's been waiting more than 4 minutes, and the announcement I posted about Saturday maintenance has 47 upvotes.
That's the dream. **Make me that.**
